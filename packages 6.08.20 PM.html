Take a ride round the most Famous Temples

Angkor Wat, Bayon, Ta Prom

From $25

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is the most famous ancient temple site in Cambodia. With its 5 lotus-like towers rising 65 meters into the sky, it is an awe-inspiring sight. Located in Siem Reap, this UNESCO World Heritage site was once the largest pre-industrial city in the world.

Bayon

The Bayon Temple, which was constructed during Jayavarman VII's reign, is unique as it is the only Buddhist state temple and the last to be built in the Angkor Empire (also sometimes called the Khmer Empire).

Ta Prom

Originally called the 'Royal Monastery' you may find this temple familiar. It was chosen as the setting for the film 'Tomb Raider', because of the large tree's growing from the ruins.

Preah Khan, Neak Pean, Ta Som,
East Mebo, Prea Rup

From $25

Preah Khan

Also known as the holy sword, this temple was built on the site of a victorious battle with the Cham in 1191.

Neak Pean

On a man-made island, named the 'entwined serpents', stands a ancient temple. The surrounding waters, were once thought to have healing powers.

Ta Som

Dedicated to his father, Jayavarman VII built this temple at the end of the 12th century. It is home to a sacred fig, which is growing around the ruins.

East Mebo

Home to 2 meter high stone elephants, this Hindu site was built in the mid 10th century. This temple is a man-made island once surrounded by a vast amount of water.

Prea Rup

Thought to be a temple for funerals, where the ashes would be rotated during the service, earning its name, 'turn the body'.
